A bicycle, also known as a bike?

A bicycle, also known as a bike, is a two-wheeled vehicle that is powered by pedals and ridden by a person: Parts A bicycle has a frame with two wheels, one in front of the other, handlebars for steering, a saddle for the rider, and pedals for propulsion. How it works The rider turns the pedals, which are attached to cranks and a chainwheel. A chain connects the chainwheel to a sprocket on the rear wheel, transmitting power. Uses Bicycles are used for transportation, recreation, and sport. They are an efficient way to convert human energy into mobility. History The first bicycle with a pedal crank was invented in 1853 by Philipp Moritz Fischer.

Yamaha YZF-R15

The Yamaha YZF-R15 is a single-cylinder sport bike made by Yamaha Motor Company in 2008.[1] In September 2011, the second iteration, called v2.0, was released in India,[2] and in April 2014 it was released in Indonesia.[3] In January 2017, the bike's third iteration, v3.0, was launched in Indonesia. Specifications The engine for the first and second iteration (v2.0) was a 149.5 cc single cylinder four-stroke engine with four valves and a single overhead camshaft. The bore and stroke were 57.0 mm × 58.7 mm (2.24 in × 2.31 in). This engine had a claimed 12.2 kW (16.4 hp) of power at 8,500 rpm and 14.5 N⋅m (10.7 lbf⋅ft) of torque at 7,500 rpm.
